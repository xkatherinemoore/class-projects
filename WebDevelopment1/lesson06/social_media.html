Social media is a means for people to interact with others over the Internet. Different social media websites have different approaches or a focus to that interaction. One of the main appeals to social media is that anyone can make an account or profile, and most social media websites do not require extensive or specialized computer knowledge to use.

Examples of Social Media

Facebook
Facebook was originally meant to be a way to interact with people you know in real life and share life events or updates. In recent years, it has expanded to also allow for business pages, the Marketplace to list items for sale locally, groups to meet people with common interests, and even dating.

Source: Facebook Brand Resources
Instagram
Instagram provides a means to share pictures (and more recently videos) online. Like Facebook, Instagram was originally used mainly to interact with people you knew in real life, but public pages for businesses as well as individuals have become incredibly common.

Source: Instagram Brand Resources
LinkedIn®
LinkedIn is a platform used for professional networking and achievements. Individual profiles are essentially a public resume that can be viewed by other professionals. LinkedIn also offers professional development through LinkedIn Learning as well as job postings and company pages.

Source: LinkedIn Brand Guidelines
